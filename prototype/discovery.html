Why Analog Still Matters.

In a world of nanometer transistors, the warm glow of a vacuum tube represents a human connection to physics that we've largely lost. These devices don't just amplify sound; they color it with a warmth that digital algorithms still struggle to replicate.

Today's "geek-out" explores the engineers in small workshops across the globe who are keeping this 1940s technology alive for high-end audio and specialized radar systems. It's a testament to the fact that "better" isn't always digital.
